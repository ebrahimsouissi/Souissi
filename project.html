Kerkannah Logo

A brand mark inspired by the Kerkennah Islands — a small archipelago off the eastern coast of Tunisia known for its fishermen, palm groves, and quiet light.

Project Personal · Speculative
Year 2023
Services Logo, Brand Mark
Tools Illustrator

The idea.

The Kerkennah Islands are one of those places that reward attention. They're small, low, and easy to miss on a map, but they have a particular character — soft horizons, weathered boats, palm shadows, and a slower kind of life. I wanted to design a brand mark that captured that feeling without leaning on the obvious tourism clichés.

The approach.

The mark is built around a single circular form — a sun, a lens, a porthole — paired with a custom-feeling wordmark. The geometry is clean but not sterile; the spacing is tight but breathable. The goal was something that could live equally on a fish-shop sign, a bottle of olive oil, or an Instagram post.

This was a speculative project — built for myself, not a client — to explore how a place-based brand could feel rooted without being kitschy. It's the kind of work I love: quiet, considered, and rooted in a real place.

What I learned.

Logo work taught me that restraint is the hardest skill. Anyone can add. Subtraction is what makes a mark last. Every version of this logo had something taken away from the previous one, until what was left was the smallest set of shapes that still meant Kerkennah to me.
