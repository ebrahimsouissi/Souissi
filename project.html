Kerkennah Brand System

A complete visual identity inspired by the Kerkennah Islands — a small archipelago off Tunisia's eastern coast, known for its fishermen, palm groves, and quiet horizons.

Project Personal · Brand System
Year 2023
Deliverables Mark, Pattern, Mockups
Tools Illustrator, Photoshop

The place.

The Kerkennah Islands sit just off the eastern coast of Tunisia — small, low, easy to miss on a map, but full of character. Soft horizons, weathered fishing boats, palm shadows, and a slower kind of life. I wanted to design a brand identity that captured that feeling without leaning on the obvious tourism clichés.

The system.

Rather than designing a single logo, I built a flexible brand language: a primary mark featuring a stylized palm tree contained in an arched window, plus a secondary "wave" mark that shares the same shape DNA. Both can carry the brand on their own, or work together across applications.

The mark needed to live equally on a fish-shop sign, a piece of hospitality merchandise, or a digital touchpoint. So the line weight is consistent, the geometry is simple enough to scale down to a label, and the silhouette holds up at any size.

The pattern.

To extend the brand beyond the logo, I designed a secondary pattern system using stylized illustrations of local sea life: octopus, whale, crab, jellyfish, seahorse, coral, and shells. Arranged in a triangular two-tone grid, the pattern gives the brand texture and personality without overwhelming the mark itself.

This is the kind of detail that separates a logo from an identity. The pattern is what lets the brand feel alive on a t-shirt, a beanie, or a piece of packaging — the world the logo lives in.

What I learned.

Building Kerkennah taught me that a brand isn't a mark — it's a kit of parts. The logo is the front door, but the pattern, the color palette, the photography style, and the mockup language are what make a brand feel like a real place rather than a single image. Every project since has started with this question: what's the world this brand lives in?
