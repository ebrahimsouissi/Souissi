Maison Solène

A complete brand identity and digital presence for an emerging maison de couture based between Tunis and Paris.

Client Maison Solène
Year 2026
Services Identity, Web, Print
Role Lead Designer

The brief.

Maison Solène approached me three months before their first runway show with a request that's familiar but rarely simple: build a brand that feels heritage without being old. They had a name, a co-founder duo, and twelve looks. They needed everything else.

The challenge wasn't aesthetic — it was tonal. Most luxury fashion brands lean cold, distant, and architectural. Maison Solène's collections were intimate, hand-finished, and rooted in North African textile traditions. The branding had to honor that warmth without sliding into folk pastiche.

The approach.

I built the system around a single typographic decision: a custom-drawn ligature for the "S" in Solène, derived from the loop of a traditional aklam brushstroke. From there, the rest of the identity pulled in tight: a single typeface family (Editorial New), a restrained palette of bone, ink, and oxblood, and a photographic direction that emphasized texture over polish.

The website was built as a digital lookbook rather than a storefront — designed to be read, not skimmed. Every page is a chapter. Every product is a small story.

The outcome.

Maison Solène's first collection sold through pre-orders in eleven days. Press coverage in L'Officiel Arabia and Vogue Italia followed the runway. More importantly, the team reported that customers were quoting brand language back to them — which is the closest thing to a quiet win you get in this work.
